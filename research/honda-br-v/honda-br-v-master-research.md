# Honda BR-V — Master Research

## 1. OVERVIEW

Honda BR-V (Bold Runabout Vehicle) is a 7-seater Low SUV produced by Honda for the Indonesian market. The current generation (gen-2, code DG3) launched in September 2021, replacing the Mobilio-based gen-1. Unlike its predecessor, gen-2 is built on a dedicated platform with proper SUV proportions.

**Target Buyer:** Families seeking a 7-seater with SUV stance, strong engine performance, and available ADAS — willing to pay premium over LMPV-based competitors.

**Market Position:** Positioned as the only genuine SUV-bodied vehicle in the Low SUV segment with available ADAS (Honda Sensing). Competes against Toyota Rush/Daihatsu Terios (RWD, no ADAS), Suzuki XL7 Hybrid (hybrid, DSBS), Mitsubishi Xpander Cross (LMPV-based, Mi-PILOT), and Hyundai Stargazer X (SmartSense).

**Key Distinction:** N7X Edition is purely a cosmetic package — no mechanical or feature differences. Honda Sensing ADAS is ONLY available on the top variant (Prestige with Honda Sensing).

## 5. STRENGTHS & WEAKNESSES

### 5.1 Strengths

1. **Best engine power in class** — 121 PS vs ~104-110 PS competitors. 0-100 km/h in 10.9-11.6s (best in LSUV/LMPV). Sources: Otodriver, Motomobi, GridOto.

2. **Best handling and driving dynamics** — Responsive steering, stable cornering, fun to drive character. Multiple reviewers rate it best in class. Source: Fitra Eri conclusion: "Performa, suspensi dan fitur safety yang TERBAIK di kelasnya."

3. **Strong hill climb performance** — Passed GridOto's 17° incline test with full load (7 pax), AC on, from stop. Owners confirm capability on Sitinjau Lauik, Puncak, and other mountain roads. Far superior to Veloz which famously failed same test.

4. **Honda Sensing ADAS available** — Only Low SUV with available ADAS in Indonesia. Real-world life-saving reports from owners (CMBS preventing crashes).

5. **Practical 7-seater with high ground clearance** — 220mm GC, row 3 folds completely flat for 564.7L cargo, adequate row 3 for adults on short trips.

6. **Fuel efficient for class** — 13-15 km/L combined real-world, 15-17 km/L highway on RON 92 fuel.

7. **Good resale value** — ~20% depreciation in 2 years, Honda badge strong in used market.

8. **Timeless design** — Multiple reviewers note design won't look dated quickly.

### 5.2 Weaknesses

1. **Highest price in class** — Base starts at Rp 297.3M, Honda Sensing at Rp 362-370M. Competitors offer more for less.

2. **NVH (cabin noise)** — Tire noise, wind noise at highway speeds, rain noise. Improved over gen-1 but still not class-leading. Fitra Eri, Otodriver, Motomobi all note this.

3. **No telescopic steering** — Universal complaint from every major reviewer. Only tilt adjustment available.

4. **No rear defogger** — Fitra Eri: "feels like a cheap car" on most expensive car in segment.

5. **Manual parking brake** — No EPB on any variant. Multiple reviewers want electric parking brake.

6. **Rear drum brakes only** — Front ventilated disc, rear drum. Rush/Terios have 4-disc (at least on some variants).

7. **No 360° camera** — LaneWatch only (left-side camera on Prestige HS). Competitors like Stargazer offer 360° camera.

8. **Honda Sensing ONLY on top variant** — At Rp 362-370M, ADAS is only available on the most expensive variant. E CVT and Prestige CVT — the volume sellers — have no ADAS.

9. **Heavy tailgate** — Must press firmly to close; won't latch with normal push. Fitra Eri noted this.

10. **Higher tax than competitors** — Rp 5.6-7M annual vs Rp 4.2-5.5M for competitors.

11. **ACC not full Stop & Go** — Must press pedal from complete stop, unlike Stargazer Cartenz HSS's SCC with Stop & Go.

## 7. REVIEWER INSIGHTS (Narrative)

### Fitra Eri (1-month ownership, -RppMftdEXg):

> Fitra Eri bought a BR-V Prestige with Honda Sensing personally. After 1 month and 1,000 km, he highlighted the lack of telescopic steering as his #1 complaint — "steering position a bit too far for my build." The absence of rear defogger on the most expensive car in its segment "feels like a cheap car." The tailgate is the heaviest he's ever experienced in this class. On the positive side, he praised the driving dynamics, Honda Sensing functionality, and overall build quality. His conclusion at Otodriver: "Performance, suspension, and safety features are THE BEST in the class right now."

### Motomobi (7uZKzwy5sMc, 1.07M views):

> The Motomobi team borrowed Fitra's BR-V for review. Despite the clickbait title "TIDAK SESUAI EKSPEKTASI," the review was largely positive. They confirmed: engine 121 PS/145 Nm, DOHC dual overhead cam, FWD with CVT. Row 3 folds flat with adjustable floor height — "kami suka yang praktis." Noted AC double blower positioned at row 2 center (doesn't directly reach row 3), leather seats are synthetic and slippery. Acceleration measured 0-100 km/h in 10.9 seconds (AC on) — impressive for the class. They concluded it's a proper SUV design rather than a raised LMPV.

### GridOto Hill Climb Test (ehfsGs-SJDM, 288K views):

> GridOto tested the BR-V Prestige HS on a 17-degree incline with full passengers (7), AC on, starting from a complete stop. The BR-V passed convincingly. This was directly compared to the Toyota Veloz, which famously failed the same test in a prior GridOto video. The result cemented BR-V's reputation as the class leader for hill climb capability. Host Trybowo Laksono noted the Honda Sensing semi-autonomous features add further value.

### B Channel (hFIYnd0hHrM, Mar 2026):

> B Channel reviewed the 2026 "Improvement" version of BR-V Prestige HS N7X Edition. Comments were mixed: "over price" (14 likes), "sama kayak taksi" (30 likes comparing to fleet cars), and "bisa dapet Fortuner Seken" (10 likes). ## 9. COMMON PROBLEMS & COMPLAINTS

1. **Suspension/undercarriage wear** — Drive shaft rubber boots, arm bushings, shock absorber rubber mounts deteriorate. More common on gen-1 but reported at higher mileage on gen-2. Source: GridOto Seken (specialist mechanic reports), Pengepulmobil.

2. **CVT "ngeden"/rubber-band effect** — Delay when kickdown, especially on steep inclines or hard acceleration. Common Honda CVT characteristic, not specific to BR-V. Source: Multiple owner reports, web reviews.

3. **Cabin noise (NVH)** — Wind noise at highway speed, rain noise, tire noise. Improved over gen-1 but still not class-leading. Source: Fitra Eri, Otodriver, Kompas.

4. **No telescopic steering** — Every major reviewer and many owners flag this. Makes driving position suboptimal for some body types. Universal complaint.

5. **No rear defogger** — Surprising omission on most expensive LSUV. Source: Fitra Eri personal ownership review.

6. **Manual parking brake** — No EPB on any variant. Multiple reviewers and owners want electric parking brake.

7. **Rear drum brakes** — Only front has disc brakes. Some competitors offer 4-disc.

8. **Heavy tailgate** — Needs firm press to close properly. Source: Fitra Eri.

9. **AC insufficient (some units)** — Reports of AC not cold enough, especially in hot climate with full passengers. Source: Hondaisme, Mycarly.

10. **Electrical issues** — Some reports of lights failing, battery drain, blown fuses. Less common. Source: Hondaisme.

## 11. FINAL INSIGHT

Honda BR-V occupies a unique position as the only genuine SUV-bodied vehicle in the Low SUV segment with ADAS capability. Its 121 PS engine, best-in-class handling, and proven hill climb performance make it the choice for buyers who prioritize driving dynamics and active safety.

**Choose BR-V if:**
- Engine power and hill climb performance are top priorities
- You value driving dynamics and handling over ride softness
- Honda Sensing ADAS is important (budget for Prestige HS)
- You want a genuine SUV form factor, not a raised LMPV
- Fun to drive matters for your daily commute

**Avoid BR-V if:**
- Budget-sensitive — competitors offer more features for less money
- NVH/cabin quietness is a priority (Xpander Cross, Stargazer are better)
- You need 360° camera or comprehensive ADAS on mid-tier variants
- Annual tax sensitivity (BR-V tax is highest in class)
- Fuel efficiency is paramount (Veloz Hybrid vastly more efficient)

**Best value variant:** E CVT (Rp 318.4M) — balances features (smart entry, remote start, TFT cluster, CVT) without paying Prestige/Sensing premium. For N7X styling at minimal premium, N7X E CVT (Rp 325.5M).

**Worth the premium:** Prestige with Honda Sensing (Rp 362.4M) — if ADAS, 6 airbags, LaneWatch, and 17" wheels matter. The Honda Sensing has demonstrably saved lives in real-world incidents reported by owners.
