# Honda BR-V — Variant Comparison

## 1. VARIANT OVERVIEW

Honda BR-V is available in 8 variants: 5 standard and 3 N7X Edition. All share the same 1.5L DOHC i-VTEC engine (121 PS / 145 Nm), FWD drivetrain, dimensions (4,490 × 1,780 × 1,685 mm), wheelbase (2,700 mm), ground clearance (220 mm), and 42L fuel tank.

N7X Edition is purely cosmetic — adds aero kit, dark chrome garnish, black interior, exclusive colors. No mechanical or feature differences from corresponding standard variant.

## 6. CRITICAL COMPARISON POINTS

### 6.1 The ADAS Gap

The biggest criticism of BR-V's variant strategy: Honda Sensing is ONLY available on the most expensive variant (Rp 362-370M). This means:

- E CVT (Rp 318.4M) — likely the volume seller — has ZERO ADAS
- Prestige CVT (Rp 342.4M) — upper mid — has ZERO ADAS
- Only Prestige HS (Rp 362.4M+) gets Honda Sensing

**For comparison:**
- Suzuki XL7 Hybrid Alpha (Rp ~305M) includes DSBS ADAS
- Hyundai Stargazer Cartenz Smart HSS (Rp 332.9M) includes full SmartSense
- Mitsubishi Xpander Cross Premium Package (Rp ~375M) includes Mi-PILOT

Buyers wanting ADAS on a BR-V must budget Rp 362M minimum. This is a significant disadvantage compared to competitors who offer ADAS from Rp 305-333M.

### 6.2 The N7X Value Proposition

N7X is purely cosmetic. For Rp 1-8M more, you get:
- Aero kit and dark chrome garnish
- Black interior theme
- Exclusive colors

For most buyers, this is a personal styling preference with no practical benefit. The premium is small enough that it won't significantly impact ownership cost, but it also delivers zero functional value.

### 6.3 S M/T: Too Basic?

The S M/T at Rp 297.3M lacks:
- Smart entry (keyless entry without pressing button)
- TFT cluster display
- LED foglamp
- Walk-away auto lock

For Rp 15.5M more, the E M/T addresses all these gaps. The S M/T is likely targeted at fleet/commercial buyers or extremely budget-conscious families. For personal use, E M/T is the recommended minimum.

### 6.4 Prestige CVT vs Prestige HS: The Rp 20M Question

The jump from Prestige CVT to Prestige HS costs Rp 20M and delivers:
- Honda Sensing (6 ADAS features)
- 2 additional airbags (4 → 6)
- LaneWatch camera

Is Rp 20M worth it? **Yes, if:**
- You frequently drive on highways/toll roads (ACC + LKAS + CMBS)
- Family safety is paramount (6 airbags vs 4, CMBS has saved lives per owner reports)
- You want semi-autonomous features for fatigue reduction

**No, if:**
- Budget is tight and Rp 342M is already stretching
- You primarily drive in city/urban where ADAS benefits are limited
- You plan to resell within 2-3 years (Prestige already has good resale)

## 8. FINAL RECOMMENDATION

For most buyers, the choice comes down to two variants:

1. **E CVT (Rp 318.4M)** — Best value for daily family use. Gets CVT comfort, smart features, and all essential safety. Skip if ADAS is critical.

2. **Prestige with Honda Sensing (Rp 362.4M)** — Best overall package for safety-conscious families. Honda Sensing has been proven to save lives. The Rp 44M premium over E CVT buys ADAS + leather + 17" + 6 airbags — a significant upgrade.

The Rp 44M gap between these two reflects Honda's variant strategy: a big jump from "comfortable daily driver" to "premium safety flagship." There's no mid-tier ADAS option, which remains BR-V's biggest variant strategy weakness.
